Q1 – Matrix Multiplication

Two matrices given — compute the product. Always write sizes first.

💡
Golden Rule$(m\times n)\cdot(n\times p)=(m\times p)$. Inner dimensions must match.

The 3 Cases

Case 1 — Column×Row = Matrix (3×1)(1×3)→(3×3)

$$\begin{pmatrix}a\\b\\c\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}d&e&f\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}ad&ae&af\\bd&be&bf\\cd&ce&cf\end{pmatrix}$$

Each row of left times the full row vector.

Case 2 — Row×Column = Scalar (1×3)(3×1)→scalar

$$[a,b,c]\begin{pmatrix}d\\e\\f\end{pmatrix}=ad+be+cf$$

Case 3 — (3×3)(3×2)=(3×2) full product

Entry $(i,j)$ = row $i$ of left DOT col $j$ of right. Compute all 6 entries.

⚠️
Common Mistakes1. Incompatible sizes    2. $AB\neq BA$ in general    3. Col×col is undefined

Q3 – Domain of a Function

Find all $x$ (or $(x,y)$) where the function is defined.

The 4 Domain Rules

Rule 1 — $\sqrt{f(x)}$ needs $f(x)\geq 0$

If in denominator $\frac{1}{\sqrt{f}}$, need $f(x)>0$ strictly.

Rule 2 — $\ln f(x)$ or $\log f(x)$ needs $f(x)>0$

Must be strictly positive — not zero, not negative.

Rule 3 — $\frac{1}{f(x)}$ needs $f(x)\neq 0$

Exclude all roots of denominator.

Rule 4 — $\frac{1}{\ln f(x)}$ needs TWO conditions

$f(x)>0$ AND $f(x)\neq 1$ (because $\ln 1=0$ → division by zero).

Sign Chart Method (for polynomials)

Factor the polynomial
Find roots, mark on number line
Test one point per interval
Domain = intervals where condition satisfied

Example: $x^3-x>0$ (Jan 2026)

Factor: $x(x-1)(x+1)=0$ → roots $-1,0,1$

IntervalSign
$(-\infty,-1)$−
$(-1,0)$+
$(0,1)$−
$(1,+\infty)$+
$$D=(-1,0)\cup(1,+\infty)$$
f(x) = x³ − x, positive regions shaded

2D Domain Patterns

$\sqrt{x^2+y^2+A}$, $A>0$Always $\geq A>0$All $\mathbb{R}^2$

Q4 – Derivatives

💡
Simplify first! $\ln(e^x)=x$ and $e^{\ln x}=x$. Always simplify before differentiating — saves a chain rule.

Quotient Rule

$$\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)'=\frac{u'v-uv'}{v^2}$$
⚠
MINUS in numerator. Most common mistake.

Product Rule

$$(uv)'=u'v+uv'$$

Key Derivatives

$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^u]=e^u\cdot u'\qquad\frac{d}{dx}[\ln u]=\frac{u'}{u}\qquad\frac{d}{dx}[\log u]=\frac{u'}{u\ln 10}$$

All Past Exam Q4s

Q4Jan 2025$f(x)=\frac{2x^2}{x^2+2}$. Find $f'(x)$.▼

$u=2x^2$, $u'=4x$; $v=x^2+2$, $v'=2x$.

$$f'(x)=\frac{4x(x^2+2)-2x^2(2x)}{(x^2+2)^2}=\frac{8x}{(x^2+2)^2}$$
Q4Mar 2025$k(y)=\frac{e^y+4y}{y^2-5}$. Find $k'(y)$.▼

$u=e^y+4y$, $u'=e^y+4$; $v=y^2-5$, $v'=2y$.

$$k'(y)=\frac{(e^y+4)(y^2-5)-(e^y+4y)(2y)}{(y^2-5)^2}$$
Q4Mar 2026$k(x)=\ln(e^x)$. Find $k'(x)$.▼
🔑
Simplify first: $\ln(e^x)=x$, so $k(x)=x$ and $k'(x)=\boxed{1}$.
Q4May 2026$k(x)=\ln(e^{2x})$. Find $k'(x)$.▼
🔑
Simplify first: $\ln(e^{2x})=2x$, so $k'(x)=\boxed{2}$.

Q5 – Second Derivative

⚠️
Most common Q5 mistake: forgetting to use product rule for $f''$. After getting $f'$, it is almost always a product — always apply product rule.

Standard Pattern: $f(x)=e^{ax^2}$

$$f'(x)=2ax\cdot e^{ax^2}$$

$f'$ is a product $(2ax)\cdot(e^{ax^2})$, so product rule for $f''$:

$$f''(x)=e^{ax^2}\cdot(4a^2x^2+2a)$$

All Past Exam Q5s

Q5Jan 2025$f(x)=e^{-x^2}$. Find $f''(x)$.▼
$f'=-2x\,e^{-x^2}$
Product rule: $f''=(-2)e^{-x^2}+(-2x)(-2x\,e^{-x^2})$
$$f''(x)=e^{-x^2}(4x^2-2)$$
Q5Mar 2025$m(x)=e^{-4x^2}$. Find $m''(x)$.▼
$m'=-8x\,e^{-4x^2}$
$m''=-8e^{-4x^2}+(-8x)(-8x\,e^{-4x^2})$
$$m''(x)=e^{-4x^2}(64x^2-8)$$
Q5Mar 2026$m(x)=e^{-x}$. Find $m''(x)$.▼
$$m'=-e^{-x},\quad m''=\boxed{e^{-x}}$$
Q5May 2026$m(x)=e^{2x}$. Find $m''(x)$.▼

Q6 – Determinant & Trace

Trace

$$\text{tr}(M)=\sum_i M_{ii}$$

Sum of diagonal entries. Always compute first — takes 5 seconds.

2×2 Det

$$\det\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix}=ad-bc$$

3×3 Det — Cofactor Expansion (row 1)

$$\det\begin{pmatrix}a&b&c\\d&e&f\\g&h&i\end{pmatrix}=a(ei-fh)-b(di-fg)+c(dh-eg)$$
⚡
Signs: $+,-,+$. Always expand along the row/column with the MOST zeros.
⚡
Shortcut 1: Any row or column of zeros → $\det=0$ instantly.
⚡
Shortcut 2: Two identical rows → $\det=0$ instantly.
⚡
Shortcut 3: Triangular matrix → $\det$ = product of diagonal entries.

All Past Exam Q6s

Q6Jan 2025$M=\begin{pmatrix}3&2&3\\0&0&0\\0&3&0\end{pmatrix}$. Find det and trace.▼

Row 2 all zeros → $\det=\boxed{0}$ instantly. Trace $=3+0+0=\boxed{3}$.

Q6Mar 2025$C=\begin{pmatrix}1&0&5\\5&4&4\\0&0&5\end{pmatrix}$. Find det and trace.▼

Trace $=1+4+5=\boxed{10}$. Expand col 3 (two zeros): $\det=5\cdot\det\binom{1\;0}{5\;4}=5\cdot4=\boxed{20}$.

Q6Mar 2026$C=\begin{pmatrix}2&1&0\\0&3&0\\1&-1&4\end{pmatrix}$. Find det and trace.▼

Trace $=2+3+4=\boxed{9}$. Expand col 3: $\det=4\cdot\det\binom{2\;1}{0\;3}=4\cdot6=\boxed{24}$.

Q6May 2026$C=\begin{pmatrix}1&0&2\\0&4&0\\-1&1&3\end{pmatrix}$. Find det and trace.▼

Trace $=1+4+3=\boxed{8}$. Row 1 expansion: $1(12-0)-0+2(0+4)=12+8=\boxed{20}$.

Q8 – Eigenvalues of the Hessian

Hessian Matrix of $f(x,y)$

$$H=\begin{pmatrix}f_{xx}&f_{xy}\\f_{xy}&f_{yy}\end{pmatrix}$$

Eigenvalues (2×2)

$$\lambda_{1,2}=\frac{\text{tr}(H)\pm\sqrt{\text{tr}(H)^2-4\det(H)}}{2}$$
⚡
Diagonal shortcut: if $f_{xy}=0$, eigenvalues are just $f_{xx}$ and $f_{yy}$. No formula needed!

All Past Exam Q8s

Q8Jan 2025$f(x,y)=x^2-y^2-2x-2y$. Eigenvalues of $H$ at $(77,25)$.▼

$f_{xx}=2$, $f_{yy}=-2$, $f_{xy}=0$. Diagonal → read off. (Point is irrelevant — $H$ is constant.)

$$\lambda_1=2,\quad\lambda_2=-2$$
Q8Mar 2025$r(x,y)=x^2+y^2-2x+y$. Eigenvalues at $(0,-2)$.▼

$f_{xx}=2,f_{yy}=2,f_{xy}=0$. Diagonal.

$$\lambda_1=\lambda_2=2$$
Q8Mar 2026$r(x,y)=e^{x+y}$. Hessian at $(0,0)$.▼

$f_{xx}=f_{yy}=f_{xy}=e^{x+y}$. At $(0,0)$: all $=1$.

$$H=\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&1\end{pmatrix}\Rightarrow\lambda_1=2,\;\lambda_2=0$$

tr$=2$, det$=0$.

Q8May 2026$r(x,y)=e^{x-y}$. Hessian at $(0,0)$.▼

$f_{xx}=e^{x-y},f_{yy}=e^{x-y},f_{xy}=-e^{x-y}$. At $(0,0)$:

$$H=\begin{pmatrix}1&-1\\-1&1\end{pmatrix}\Rightarrow\lambda_1=2,\;\lambda_2=0$$

Exercise B – 2D Optimisation

Find and classify all critical points of $g(x,y)$, then write Taylor expansion.

The 6-Step Method

Domain $D_g$ — usually $\mathbb{R}^2$ unless log/sqrt
Gradient — compute $g_x$ and $g_y$
Critical Points — solve $g_x=0$ AND $g_y=0$. Find ALL.
Hessian $H$ — compute $g_{xx}$, $g_{yy}$, $g_{xy}$
Classify using $\det(H)$ and sign of $g_{xx}$
Taylor 2nd order at each critical point
Compute $\det(H)=g_{xx}g_{yy}-g_{xy}^2$
$\det H<0$ → SADDLE POINT
$\det H>0$ & $g_{xx}>0$ → LOCAL MIN
$\det H>0$ & $g_{xx}<0$ → LOCAL MAX
$\det H=0$ → INCONCLUSIVE
$$g(x,y)\approx g(x^*,y^*)+\frac{1}{2}\left[g_{xx}(x-x^*)^2+2g_{xy}(x-x^*)(y-y^*)+g_{yy}(y-y^*)^2\right]$$
⚠️
Do NOT forget the ½ factor. Cross term coefficient is $2g_{xy}$, not $g_{xy}$.
💡
Separable $g=f(x)+h(y)$: $g_{xy}=0$ always → diagonal Hessian. Solve $g_x=0$ independently for $x$, $g_y=0$ for $y$. CPs = all combinations. If each gives 3 roots → 9 CPs total!

⚡ Cheat Sheet

Read this the morning of the exam. 12 things you must know cold.

1
Matrix Mult: $(m\times n)\cdot(n\times p)=(m\times p)$. Entry$(i,j)$ = row $i$ DOT col $j$.
2
Log/Exp cancel: $e^{\ln x}=x$, $\ln(e^x)=x$, $\ln 1=0$, $e^0=1$. ALWAYS simplify before calc.
3
Domain rules: sqrt $\geq0$; log $>0$; fraction $\neq0$; $1/\ln$: arg $>0$ AND $\neq1$.
4
Quotient rule: $(u/v)'=(u'v-uv')/v^2$. MINUS in numerator.
5
Product rule: $(uv)'=u'v+uv'$. Use for $f''$ when $f'$ is a product.
6
Key derivs: $[e^u]'=e^u u'$; $[\ln u]'=u'/u$; $[\log u]'=u'/(u\ln10)$.
7
Det shortcuts: Zero row/col: $\det=0$. Triangular: $\det$ = product of diagonal.
8
1D min/max: $f'=0$, then $f''>0\Rightarrow$ min, $f''<0\Rightarrow$ max. Quadratic: $x^*=-b/2a$.
9
Exercise A: Domain → Limits/Asymptotes → $f'$ sign → $f''$ sign → Sketch.
10
Exercise B: $\nabla g=0$ for CPs. $\det H>0,g_{xx}>0$=MIN; $\det H>0,g_{xx}<0$=MAX; $\det H<0$=SADDLE.
11
Taylor 2D: $g(x^*,y^*)+\frac{1}{2}[g_{xx}\Delta x^2+2g_{xy}\Delta x\Delta y+g_{yy}\Delta y^2]$. Never forget $\frac{1}{2}$.
12
Separable: $g=f(x)+h(y)\Rightarrow g_{xy}=0$. Diagonal $H$. If 3 $x$-roots $\times$ 3 $y$-roots = 9 CPs.

Top 8 Mistakes to Avoid

❌
NOT simplifying $\ln(e^x)=x$ before differentiatingFix: Simplify first, every time
❌
$+$ instead of $-$ in quotient ruleFix: $(u'v - uv')/v^2$ — it is MINUS
❌
No product rule for $f''(x)$Fix: $f'(x)$ is almost always a product
❌
Not checking zero rows for determinantFix: Zero row → $\det=0$ instantly
❌
Missing critical points in Exercise BFix: If $g_x=0$ has 3 sols AND $g_y=0$ has 3 → 9 CPs
❌
$\det>0$ means min without checking $g_{xx}$Fix: Need $\det>0$ AND $g_{xx}>0$ for min
❌
Forgetting $\frac{1}{2}$ in TaylorFix: $g+\frac{1}{2}[\ldots]$ not $g+[\ldots]$
❌
