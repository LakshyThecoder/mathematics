Q1 – Matrix Multiplication

Every exam: two matrices given, compute the product. Always write sizes first.

💡
Golden Rule$(m\times n)\cdot(n\times p)=(m\times p)$. Inner dims must match.

The 3 Cases That Appear

Case 1 — Column (3×1) × Row (1×3) = Matrix (3×3)

$$\begin{pmatrix}a\\b\\c\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}d&e&f\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}ad&ae&af\\bd&be&bf\\cd&ce&cf\end{pmatrix}$$
📝
Jan/Dec 2025 & Mar/Dec 2025: $M=\begin{pmatrix}-5\\3\\-5\end{pmatrix}$, $N=\begin{pmatrix}0&-2&3\end{pmatrix}$ → 3×3 result. Each row = $M_i\times N$.

Case 2 — Row (1×3) × Column (3×1) = Scalar

$$[a,b,c]\begin{pmatrix}d\\e\\f\end{pmatrix}=ad+be+cf$$
📝
Jan 2026: $A=[-\frac{5}{9},\frac{1}{3},\frac{5}{9}]\cdot[0,-2,3]^T=0-\frac{2}{3}+\frac{5}{3}=1$

Case 3 — (3×3) × (3×2) = (3×2)

Compute each of the 6 entries: $R[i,j]=\text{row }i\text{ of left}\cdot\text{col }j\text{ of right}$

📝
Jul 2025: $A=\begin{pmatrix}-5&0&0\\3&1&1\\-5&1&1\end{pmatrix}$, $B=\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\-2&1\\3&1\end{pmatrix}$ → $AB=\begin{pmatrix}0&-5\\1&5\\1&-3\end{pmatrix}$
⚠️
Common Mistakes1. Multiplying incompatible sizes   2. Taking col×col instead of row×col   3. Forgetting $AB\neq BA$

Q2 – Logarithms & Exponentials

Evaluate $f(x_0)$ where $x_0$ is given as $\ln(1)$, $\log(\ldots)$, etc.

🔑
THE KEY CANCELLATIONS — memorise first$e^{\ln x}=x$ and $\ln(e^x)=x$ — they cancel completely. Use BEFORE any calculation.
y = ln(x) and y = eˣ are mirror images across y = x

Every Identity with WHY

$\ln(1)=0$ and $\log(1)=0$

Because $e^0=1$. The power you raise $e$ to in order to get 1 is 0. Whenever you see $\log(1)$ — replace with $0$ instantly.

$e^{\ln x}=x$ — THE BIG ONE

$$e^{\ln(\text{anything})}=\text{that thing}$$

Jul 2025: $g(x)=e^{\ln(1/x^2+3/x+4)}$ → cancel: $g(x)=\frac{1}{x^2}+\frac{3}{x}+4$, so $g(1)=1+3+4=\mathbf{8}$. NOT $e^8$!

$\ln(e^x)=x$

Same cancellation reversed. $k(x)=\ln(e^x)\Rightarrow k(x)=x\Rightarrow k'(x)=1$.

⚠️
Appeared in Jan 2026 and Mar 2026 Q4. Do NOT use chain rule — simplify first!

Product / Quotient / Power Rules

$$\ln(ab)=\ln a+\ln b \qquad \ln\!\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)=\ln a-\ln b \qquad \ln(a^n)=n\ln a$$

Special: $\ln(1/x)=-\ln x$

Common Log Values to Memorise

$\log(2)$$\approx 0.301$Memorise!
$\log(4)$$2\log 2\approx 0.602$$\log(2^2)=2\log 2$
$\ln(1)$$0$$e^0=1$

Q3 – Domain of a Function

Find all $x$ (or $(x,y)$) where the function is defined.

The 4 Domain Rules

Rule 1: $\sqrt{f(x)}$ requires $f(x)\geq 0$

Square root of negative is not real. Exception: if in denominator $\frac{1}{\sqrt{f}}$, need $f(x)>0$ strictly.

Rule 2: $\log f(x)$ or $\ln f(x)$ requires $f(x)>0$

Must be strictly positive — not zero, not negative.

Rule 3: $\frac{1}{f(x)}$ requires $f(x)\neq 0$

Exclude all $x$ where denominator is zero.

Rule 4: $\frac{1}{\ln f(x)}$ — TWO conditions

$f(x)>0$ AND $f(x)\neq 1$ (because $\ln(1)=0$ would divide by zero). Jan 2025: $f=\frac{1}{\ln(x^2+1)}$: $x^2+1>0$ always, but $x^2+1\neq 1\Rightarrow x\neq 0$. Domain: $\mathbb{R}\setminus\{0\}$

Sign Chart Method

When domain requires polynomial $>0$, use a sign chart:

Factor the polynomial
Find roots (set =0)
Mark roots on number line
Test ONE point per interval
Domain = intervals where positive (or ≥0 for sqrt)

Example: $x^3-x>0$ (Jan 2026 Q3)

Factor: $x(x-1)(x+1)=0$ → roots $-1,0,1$

Interval$x$$(x-1)$$(x+1)$Product

Domain (where $>0$): $(-1,0)\cup(1,+\infty)$

Q4 – Derivatives

Always simplify first (e.g. $\ln(e^x)=x$), then differentiate.

The 3 Core Rules

Quotient Rule

$$\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)'=\frac{u'v-uv'}{v^2}$$
⚠
It is MINUS in the numerator. Most common exam mistake.

Product Rule

$$(uv)'=u'v+uv'$$

Chain Rule & Key Derivatives

$$\frac{d}{dx}[e^u]=e^u\cdot u'\qquad\frac{d}{dx}[\ln u]=\frac{u'}{u}\qquad\frac{d}{dx}[\log u]=\frac{u'}{u\ln 10}$$
💡
Simplify First: $k(x)=\ln(e^x)\Rightarrow k(x)=x\Rightarrow k'(x)=1$. Much easier than chain rule!

All Q4 Past Exams — Solved

Q4Jan 2025$f(\pi)=\frac{2\pi^2}{\pi^2+2}$. Find $f'(\pi)$.▼

$u=2\pi^2$, $v=\pi^2+2$, $u'=4\pi$, $v'=2\pi$.

$$f'=\frac{4\pi(\pi^2+2)-2\pi^2(2\pi)}{(\pi^2+2)^2}=\frac{8\pi}{(\pi^2+2)^2}$$
Q4Mar/Dec 2025$k(y)=\frac{e^y+4y}{y^2-5}$. Find $k'(y)$.▼

$u=e^y+4y$, $u'=e^y+4$; $v=y^2-5$, $v'=2y$.

$$k'(y)=\frac{(e^y+4)(y^2-5)-(e^y+4y)(2y)}{(y^2-5)^2}$$
Q4Mar 2026$k(x)=\ln(e^x)$. Find $k'(x)$.▼
🔑
SIMPLIFY FIRST: $\ln(e^x)=x$, so $k(x)=x$ and $k'(x)=\boxed{1}$.
Q4May 2026$k(x)=\ln(e^{2x})$. Find $k'(x)$.▼
🔑
SIMPLIFY FIRST: $\ln(e^{2x})=2x$, so $k'(x)=\boxed{2}$.

Q5 – Second Derivative

⚠️
Most common mistake: forgetting to use the product rule for $f''(x)$. $f'(x)$ is almost always a product — always apply product rule.

Key Pattern: $f(x)=e^{ax^2}$

$f'(x)=e^{ax^2}\cdot 2ax$   (chain rule)

$f''(x)=(e^{ax^2})'\cdot 2ax+e^{ax^2}\cdot(2a)$   (product rule!)

$$f''(x)=e^{ax^2}(4a^2x^2+2a)$$

All Q5 Past Exams — Solved

Q5Jan 2025$f(x)=e^{-x^2}$. Find $f''(x)$.▼
$f'(x)=-2x\,e^{-x^2}$
$f''(x)=-2e^{-x^2}+(-2x)(-2x\,e^{-x^2})$
$$f''(x)=e^{-x^2}(4x^2-2)$$
Q5Mar/Dec 2025$m(x)=e^{-4x^2}$. Find $m''(x)$.▼
$m'(x)=-8x\,e^{-4x^2}$
$m''(x)=-8e^{-4x^2}+(-8x)(-8x\,e^{-4x^2})$
$$m''(x)=e^{-4x^2}(64x^2-8)$$
Q5Mar 2026$m(x)=e^{-x}$. Find $m''(x)$.▼

$m'=-e^{-x}$, $m''=e^{-x}$

$$m''(x)=\boxed{e^{-x}}$$
Q5May 2026$m(x)=e^{2x}$. Find $m''(x)$.▼

$m'=2e^{2x}$, $m''=4e^{2x}$

$$m''(x)=\boxed{4e^{2x}}$$

Q6 – Determinant & Trace

Trace

$$\text{tr}(M)=\sum_i M_{ii}$$

Sum of diagonal entries. Compute first — it is always fast.

2×2 Determinant

$$\det\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix}=ad-bc$$

3×3 Determinant — Cofactor Expansion (row 1)

$$\det\begin{pmatrix}a&b&c\\d&e&f\\g&h&i\end{pmatrix}=a(ei-fh)-b(di-fg)+c(dh-eg)$$

Signs: $+,-,+$. Choose the row/column with the most zeros to expand along.

Shortcut Checks — Do These FIRST

⚡
Zero row/col: any entire row or column is all zeros → $\det=0$ instantly.
⚡
Identical rows: two rows are equal → $\det=0$ instantly.
⚡
Triangular matrix: $\det$ = product of diagonal entries.

All Q6 Past Exams — Solved

Q6Jan 2025$M=\begin{pmatrix}3&2&3\\0&0&0\\0&3&0\end{pmatrix}$. Find det and trace.▼

Row 2 all zeros → $\det=\boxed{0}$ instantly. Trace $=3+0+0=\boxed{3}$.

Q6Mar/Dec 2025$C=\begin{pmatrix}1&0&5\\5&4&4\\0&0&5\end{pmatrix}$. Find det and trace.▼

Trace $=1+4+5=\boxed{10}$. Expand col 3: only $C_{33}=5$ contributes. $\det=5\cdot\det\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\5&4\end{pmatrix}=5\cdot4=\boxed{20}$.

Q6Mar 2026$C=\begin{pmatrix}2&1&0\\0&3&0\\1&-1&4\end{pmatrix}$. Find det and trace.▼

Trace $=2+3+4=\boxed{9}$. Expand col 3: only $C_{33}=4$ contributes. $\det=4\cdot\det\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\0&3\end{pmatrix}=4\cdot6=\boxed{24}$.

Q6May 2026$C=\begin{pmatrix}1&0&2\\0&4&0\\-1&1&3\end{pmatrix}$. Find det and trace.▼

Trace $=1+4+3=\boxed{8}$. Expand row 1: $1\cdot(12-0)-0+2\cdot(0+4)=12+8=\boxed{20}$.

Q7 – Local Min/Max of 1D Functions

Quadratic Shortcut

$$f(x)=ax^2+bx+c\Rightarrow x^*=-\frac{b}{2a}$$

$a>0\Rightarrow$ minimum; $a<0\Rightarrow$ maximum.

General Method

Find $f'(x)$
Solve $f'(x)=0$
$f''(x_0)>0\Rightarrow$ min; $f''(x_0)<0\Rightarrow$ max

All Q7 Past Exams — Solved

Q7Jan 2025$f(x)=x^3-4x$. Local minimum?▼

$f'=3x^2-4=0\Rightarrow x=\pm\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}$. $f''=6x$. At $x=\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}>0$: $f''>0\Rightarrow$ min.

$$x_{\min}=\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}$$
Q7Mar/Dec 2025$q(x)=x^4-10x^2$. Local minimum?▼

$q'=4x^3-20x=4x(x^2-5)=0\Rightarrow x=0,\pm\sqrt{5}$. $q''=12x^2-20$. At $x=\pm\sqrt{5}$: $q''=40>0\Rightarrow$ minima.

$$x_{\min}=\pm\sqrt{5}$$
Q7Mar 2026$q(x)=x^2-4x+1$. Minimum?▼

Vertex: $x^*=\frac{4}{2}=2$. $q(2)=4-8+1=-3$.

$$x_{\min}=2,\quad f(2)=-3$$
Q7May 2026$q(x)=x^2-6x+5$. Minimum?▼
$$x_{\min}=3,\quad q(3)=9-18+5=-4$$
Q7Dec 2024$f(x)=x^2-2x$. Minimum?▼

Vertex: $x^*=\frac{2}{2}=1$. $f(1)=1-2=-1$.

$$x_{\min}=1,\quad f(1)=-1$$

Q8 – Eigenvalues of the Hessian

Hessian Matrix

$$H=\begin{pmatrix}f_{xx}&f_{xy}\\f_{yx}&f_{yy}\end{pmatrix}$$

Eigenvalues of 2×2

$$\lambda_{1,2}=\frac{\text{tr}(H)\pm\sqrt{\text{tr}(H)^2-4\det(H)}}{2}$$
⚡
Diagonal shortcut: if $f_{xy}=0$, eigenvalues ARE the diagonal entries $f_{xx}$ and $f_{yy}$. No formula needed!

All Q8 Past Exams — Solved

Q8Jan 2025$f(x,y)=x^2-y^2-2x-2y$. Eigenvalues of $H$ at $(77,25)$.▼

$f_{xx}=2$, $f_{yy}=-2$, $f_{xy}=0$. Diagonal $H$ → eigenvalues = diagonal.

$$\lambda_1=2,\quad\lambda_2=-2$$

The point $(77,25)$ is irrelevant — $H$ is constant here!

Q8Mar/Dec 2025$r(x,y)=x^2+y^2-2x+y$. Eigenvalues of $H$ at $(0,-2)$.▼

$f_{xx}=2$, $f_{yy}=2$, $f_{xy}=0$. Diagonal.

$$\lambda_1=\lambda_2=2$$
Q8Mar 2026$r(x,y)=e^{x+y}$. Find Hessian at $(0,0)$.▼

$f_{xx}=f_{yy}=f_{xy}=e^{x+y}$. At $(0,0)$: all $=1$.

$$H(0,0)=\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\1&1\end{pmatrix}\quad\Rightarrow\quad\lambda_1=2,\;\lambda_2=0$$

tr$=2$, det$=1-1=0$. $\lambda=\frac{2\pm 2}{2}$.

Q8May 2026$r(x,y)=e^{x-y}$. Hessian at $(0,0)$.▼

$f_{xx}=e^{x-y}$, $f_{yy}=e^{x-y}$, $f_{xy}=-e^{x-y}$. At $(0,0)$:

$$H=\begin{pmatrix}1&-1\\-1&1\end{pmatrix}\quad\Rightarrow\quad\lambda_1=2,\;\lambda_2=0$$
Q8Dec 2024$f(x,y)=x^2+y^2$. Eigenvalues at $(0,0)$.▼

$f_{xx}=2$, $f_{yy}=2$, $f_{xy}=0$. Diagonal.

$$\lambda_1=\lambda_2=2$$

Exercise A – Full Function Analysis

Always follow all 5 steps. Each builds on the previous. Partial credit for each step shown.

The 5-Step Method (memorise the order)

Domain $D_f$ — apply the 4 domain rules
Limits at borders of $D_f$ and at $\pm\infty$ — find asymptotes
$f'(x)$ — compute, find sign chart → local max/min
$f''(x)$ — compute, find sign chart → inflection points, convexity
Sketch — use everything from steps 1–4

Asymptote Reference

TypeHow to FindExample
Vertical at $x=a$$\lim_{x\to a^\pm}f(x)=\pm\infty$$f=\frac{1}{x-a}$: VA at $x=a$
Horizontal $y=L$$\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}f(x)=L$$f=\frac{x}{x^2-1}$: HA $y=0$
Oblique $y=mx+b$$m=\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{f(x)}{x}$, $b=\lim[f-mx]$When deg(top)=deg(bottom)+1

f(x) = (5x²−2x) / eˣ
Domain: $D_f=\mathbb{R}$ (exp defined everywhere)
Limits: $\lim_{x\to+\infty}=0$ (exp dominates), $\lim_{x\to-\infty}=+\infty$. Horizontal asymptote $y=0$ as $x\to+\infty$.
$f'(x)$: quotient rule. $u=5x^2-2x$, $u'=10x-2$, $v=e^x$, $v'=e^x$.
$f'=\frac{(10x-2)e^x-(5x^2-2x)e^x}{e^{2x}}=\frac{-5x^2+12x-2}{e^x}$
Roots of numerator: $x=\frac{12\pm\sqrt{144-40}}{10}=\frac{12\pm\sqrt{104}}{10}\approx 0.18$ and $2.22$
Sign chart $f'$: positive between roots → increasing on $(0.18,2.22)$. Local min at $x\approx0.18$, local max at $x\approx2.22$.
Sketch: comes from $+\infty$ (left), has local min then max, asymptotes to $y=0$ from above.
f(x) = x&sup4; − 2x³ + 1
Domain: $\mathbb{R}$
Limits: $\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}=+\infty$ (even degree). No asymptotes.
$f'(x)=4x^3-6x^2=2x^2(2x-3)$. Roots: $x=0$ (double), $x=3/2$. Sign: negative on $(-\infty,3/2)$ except $x=0$ where $f'=0$. Positive on $(3/2,+\infty)$. Local min at $x=3/2$. $x=0$ is an inflection (not extremum).
$f''(x)=12x^2-12x=12x(x-1)$. Roots: $x=0,1$. Inflection points at $x=0$: $f(0)=1$ and $x=1$: $f(1)=0$.
Sketch: $f(3/2)=(3/2)^4-2(3/2)^3+1=-11/16$ is the local min. Both ends go to $+\infty$.
f(x) = ln((4−x)/(x−2))
Domain: need $\frac{4-x}{x-2}>0$. Both same sign: $(2,4)$. $D_f=(2,4)$.
Limits: $\lim_{x\to2^+}f=+\infty$, $\lim_{x\to4^-}f=-\infty$. Vertical asymptotes at $x=2$ and $x=4$.
$f'(x)$: Let $u=(4-x)/(x-2)$, $f=\ln u$. $u'=\frac{-(x-2)-(4-x)}{(x-2)^2}=\frac{-2}{(x-2)^2}$. $f'=\frac{u'}{u}=\frac{-2/(x-2)^2}{(4-x)/(x-2)}=\frac{-2}{(4-x)(x-2)}<0$ always on $(2,4)$. Strictly decreasing, no extrema.
$f''(x)$: differentiate $f'$. Inflection at $x=3$ (midpoint of domain by symmetry).
Sketch: strictly decreasing from $+\infty$ to $-\infty$ on $(2,4)$, inflection at $x=3$.
ℹ️
Dec 2024 version: $f=\ln\!\left(\frac{2-x}{x-4}\right)$ — same analysis, domain $(2,4)$.
f(x) = x⁵ − x³
Domain: $\mathbb{R}$. Odd function: $f(-x)=-f(x)$. Symmetric about origin.
Limits: $\pm\infty$ at $\pm\infty$. No asymptotes.
$f'(x)=5x^4-3x^2=x^2(5x^2-3)$. Roots: $x=0$, $x=\pm\sqrt{3/5}=\pm\frac{\sqrt{15}}{5}\approx\pm0.775$.
Sign chart: $f'<0$ on $(-\sqrt{3/5},0)\cup(0,\sqrt{3/5})$, positive outside. Local max at $x=-\sqrt{3/5}$, local min at $x=+\sqrt{3/5}$, $x=0$ is inflection.
f(x) = 1/(1−x²)
Domain: $1-x^2\neq0\Rightarrow x\neq\pm1$. $D_f=\mathbb{R}\setminus\{-1,1\}$
Limits: $\lim_{x\to\pm1}=\pm\infty$ (VAs at $x=\pm1$). $\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}f=0$ (HA: $y=0$). On $(-1,1)$: $f(0)=1>0$, goes to $+\infty$ at both ends.
$f'(x)=\frac{2x}{(1-x^2)^2}$. Zero at $x=0$. Negative for $x<0$, positive for $x>0$ (within each interval). On $(-1,1)$: local min at $x=0$, $f(0)=1$.
On outer intervals: always decreasing (or increasing), no extrema there.
f(x) = x / (x²−1)
Domain: $x^2-1\neq0\Rightarrow x\neq\pm1$
Limits: VAs at $x=\pm1$. $\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}=0$ → HA $y=0$. Odd function.

Exercise B – 2D Optimization

The 6-Step Method

Domain $D_g$ — usually $\mathbb{R}^2$ unless log/sqrt
Gradient $\nabla g$ — compute partial derivatives $g_x$ and $g_y$
Critical Points — solve $g_x=0$ AND $g_y=0$ simultaneously. Find ALL.
Hessian $H$ — compute $g_{xx}$, $g_{yy}$, $g_{xy}$
Classify using $\det(H)$ and sign of $g_{xx}$
Taylor — 2nd order expansion at each critical point

Classification Decision Tree

Evaluate $H$ at critical point $(x^*,y^*)$
Compute $\det(H)=g_{xx}g_{yy}-g_{xy}^2$
$\det(H)<0$ → SADDLE POINT (always, no exception)
$\det(H)>0$ → check $g_{xx}$:
$g_{xx}>0$ → LOCAL MINIMUM
$g_{xx}<0$ → LOCAL MAXIMUM
$\det(H)=0$ → INCONCLUSIVE

Taylor Expansion Formula

$$g(x,y)\approx g(x^*,y^*)+\frac{1}{2}\left[g_{xx}(x-x^*)^2+2g_{xy}(x-x^*)(y-y^*)+g_{yy}(y-y^*)^2\right]$$
⚠️
Do NOT forget the ½ factor. Cross term coefficient is $2g_{xy}$. Linear terms vanish because $(x^*,y^*)$ is a critical point.
💡
Separable Functions $g=f(x)+h(y)$: $g_{xy}=0$ always → diagonal Hessian. Solve $g_x=0$ for $x$ only, $g_y=0$ for $y$ only. CPs = all combinations. If each gives 3 solutions → 9 CPs total!

⚡ Cheat Sheet — Read This Morning of Exam

12 Things You Must Know Cold

1 MATRIX MULT: $(m\times n)\cdot(n\times p)=(m\times p)$. Entry$(i,j)=$row $i$ DOT col $j$.
2 $\ln(1)=0$, $e^0=1$, $e^{\ln x}=x$, $\ln(e^x)=x$. These CANCEL. Use in Q2 and Q4 FIRST.
3 DOMAIN: sqrt needs $\geq0$, log needs $>0$, fraction needs $\neq0$, $1/\ln$: $>0$ AND $\neq1$.
4 QUOTIENT: $(u'v-uv')/v^2$. PRODUCT: $u'v+uv'$. CHAIN: $f'(g)\cdot g'$. SIMPLIFY $\ln(e^x)$ FIRST.
5 SECOND DERIV: $f'(x)$ is usually a product → use PRODUCT RULE to get $f''(x)$.
6 TRACE = sum of diagonal. DETERMINANT: check zero rows FIRST ($\det=0$ instant).
7 LOCAL MIN/MAX: $f'=0$, then $f''>0\Rightarrow$ min, $f''<0\Rightarrow$ max. Quadratic: $x^*=-b/(2a)$.
8 HESSIAN: $H=\begin{pmatrix}g_{xx}&g_{xy}\\g_{xy}&g_{yy}\end{pmatrix}$. Diagonal if $g_{xy}=0$.
9 EXERCISE A: Domain → Limits → $f'$ sign chart → $f''$ sign chart → Sketch. Always.
10 EXERCISE B: $\nabla g=0$ gives CPs. $H$ at each CP. $\det>0,g_{xx}>0$=MIN; $\det>0,g_{xx}<0$=MAX; $\det<0$=SADDLE.
11 TAYLOR: $g(x^*,y^*)+\frac{1}{2}[g_{xx}\Delta x^2+2g_{xy}\Delta x\Delta y+g_{yy}\Delta y^2]$.
12 SEPARABLE $g=f(x)+h(y)$: $g_{xy}=0$ always. Diagonal $H$. If each factor has 3 roots → 9 CPs.
